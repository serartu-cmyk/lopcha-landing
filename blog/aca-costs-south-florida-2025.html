How Much Does ACA Health
Insurance Cost in South Florida 2025?

March 28, 2025
6 min read

One of the most common questions we receive at Lopcha is: "How much will I pay for ACA?" The short answer: probably less than you think, especially if you live in Miami-Dade or Broward and your income falls within certain ranges.

ACA subsidies have historically been generous for South Florida residents, and with improvements enacted by the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA), many people qualify for very low-cost or even $0 monthly premium plans.

Important fact for 2025

Thanks to expanded subsidies, 4 out of 10 people who enroll in ACA in Florida pay $10 or less per month. The average subsidy in Miami-Dade covers over 80% of the plan premium.

What is the ACA Subsidy (APTC)?

The Advanced Premium Tax Credit (APTC) is a federal government subsidy that reduces your monthly insurance premium. It is calculated based on your annual income and family size, and is applied directly to your premium — you don't have to wait for tax time to benefit.

To qualify, your income must be between 100% and 400% of the federal poverty level (FPL). But with the subsidy extension, if your income is higher, you can still receive help if the "second cheapest Silver plan" premium in your area represents more than 8.5% of your income.

Plan Levels: Bronze, Silver, Gold or Platinum?

ACA plans are divided into levels based on how costs are split between you and the insurer:

  • Bronze (60/40): Lowest premium, higher deductibles and copayments. Ideal if you're healthy and rarely use insurance. Typical deductible: $6,000–$9,000/year.
  • Silver (70/30): Balance between premium and costs. Important: Only with Silver plans can you receive the Cost-Sharing Reduction (CSR) if your income is between 100–250% FPL, dramatically lowering your deductible.
  • Gold (80/20): Higher premium, but low copayments and low deductible (~$1,000). Better if you use insurance frequently.
  • Platinum (90/10): Highest premium, but you pay very little when using insurance. Rare in Florida.

Bronze or Silver?

If your income is between 100% and 250% FPL, choose a Silver plan even if the premium is higher. The Cost-Sharing Reduction (CSR) can lower your deductible from $7,000 to $500 or less — a benefit that only applies to Silver plans.

Major ACA Insurers in South Florida (2025)

  • Ambetter (Sunshine Health) — very popular in Miami-Dade and Broward, wide network of Hispanic doctors
  • Florida Blue (BCBS) — wide network, Silver plans with good coverage
  • Molina Healthcare — affordable options for low-income families
  • Oscar Health — good app, free telemedicine, popular with young people
  • UnitedHealthcare — wide network of specialists
  • Simply Healthcare — strong presence in Spanish-speaking communities

When Can You Enroll in ACA?

  • Open Enrollment (OEP): November 1 through January 15 — main enrollment period.
  • Special Enrollment Period (SEP): If you lose your job, divorce, have a baby, move or have another qualifying event, you have 60 days to enroll outside the normal period.

What If You're Self-Employed?

If you work for yourself, ACA can be your best health insurance option. You can deduct 100% of your health insurance premium from your federal taxes, reducing your effective cost even more. Also, if your business income varies, you can estimate average income to calculate your subsidy.

Common Mistakes When Enrolling in ACA

  • Choosing only by low premium: A $0/month Bronze plan with an $8,000 deductible can be devastating if you end up in the hospital.
  • Not verifying your doctor's network: Make sure your primary care doctor and specialists are in the network before choosing.
  • Not reporting income changes: If your income changes during the year, update your information on healthcare.gov to avoid overpaying or owing money at tax time.
  • Missing out on CSR: Many low-income people choose Bronze for the low premium and miss the CSR benefits available only with Silver plans.
