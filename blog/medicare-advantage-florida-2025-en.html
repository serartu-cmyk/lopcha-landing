Medicare Advantage in 2025:
Complete Guide for Florida

April 10, 2025
8 min read
South Florida

If you are 65 or older and live in Miami, Hialeah, Doral or any city in South Florida, you've probably already heard about Medicare Advantage. But with so much information—and so much advertising—it's hard to know what's real and what's just marketing.

In this guide, we'll explain exactly what Medicare Advantage is, how it works in Florida, what plans exist, how much they cost, and how to choose the right one for your situation.

Key Point

Medicare Advantage (Part C) is a private alternative to Original Medicare. You must continue paying your Part B Premium, but Advantage plans can include dental, vision, hearing coverage and more—sometimes with a $0 monthly premium.

What is Medicare Advantage?

Medicare Advantage, also called Medicare Part C, is offered by private insurance companies approved by Medicare. These plans cover everything that Original Medicare (Parts A and B) covers, but generally add additional benefits.

In Florida, Advantage plans are extremely popular for one simple reason: competition between insurers is very high, which forces companies to offer increasingly comprehensive plans with lower premiums.

Types of Medicare Advantage Plans in Florida

HMO (Health Maintenance Organization)

HMO plans require you to use doctors within the plan's network and to have a primary care physician (PCP) to coordinate your care. Generally, they have lower premiums but less flexibility. They are the most common option in Miami-Dade and Broward.

PPO (Preferred Provider Organization)

PPO plans give you more freedom: you can see doctors outside the network (although with higher copayments). If you travel frequently or have specialists outside the network, a PPO might be better for you.

PFFS (Private Fee-for-Service)

Less common plans that determine how much they pay providers. Any provider who accepts the plan's terms can treat you.

SNP (Special Needs Plans)

Specialized plans for people with specific chronic conditions, people with dual Medicaid coverage, or nursing home residents. They are very common in South Florida given the large number of eligible people.

Major Insurers in South Florida (2025)

These are the companies with the largest presence in Miami-Dade and Broward County:

  • Humana — one of the most popular in Florida, with a wide network of Hispanic doctors
  • UnitedHealthcare — AARP Medicare Advantage plans with good dental coverage
  • Devoted Health — specialized in Florida, highly rated by Hispanic members
  • Simply Healthcare — strong presence in Spanish-speaking communities
  • Wellcare — $0 premium options with OTC and transportation benefits
  • Cigna / Cigna-HealthSpring — good network of specialists in Miami

How Much Does Medicare Advantage Cost in Florida?

One of the biggest advantages of Medicare Advantage in Florida is that many plans have a $0 premium. But "free" doesn't mean no cost: what matters is the total cost when you use the insurance.

Costs to Consider (2025)

  • Plan monthly premium$0 – $80/month
  • PCP doctor visit copay$0 – $15
  • Specialist copay$20 – $50
  • Urgent care copay$50 – $120
  • Annual out-of-pocket maximum$3,000 – $8,850

Additional Benefits Most Common in Florida

In addition to basic medical coverage, Advantage plans in Florida usually include:

  • Preventive and major dental — cleanings, extractions, crowns and even dentures
  • Vision — eye exams and discounts on glasses or frames
  • Hearing — hearing aids with partial or full coverage
  • OTC (Over-the-Counter) — monthly credit of $25–$100 to buy non-prescription medications, vitamins and health products
  • Medical transportation — rides to medical appointments, especially useful in Miami-Dade
  • Gym membership — access to Silver Sneakers or similar
  • Meals after hospitalization — some insurers cover home-delivered meals
  • Telemedicine — virtual consultations at no additional cost

When Can You Enroll?

Enrollment periods are crucial. You cannot change plans at any time:

  • IEP (Initial Enrollment Period): The 3 months before and 3 months after you turn 65 — your best time to enroll.
  • AEP (Annual Enrollment Period): October 15 through December 7 — you can change, join, or leave an Advantage plan.
  • OEP (Open Enrollment Period): January 1 through March 31 — if you already have Advantage, you can switch once to another Advantage plan or return to Original Medicare.
  • SEP (Special Enrollment Period): If you move, lose other coverage, or have a qualifying event, you can enroll outside normal periods.

How to Choose the Right Plan?

There is no universal "best plan." Your choice depends on your particular situation. Ask yourself these questions:

  • Are your current doctors in the network? Check that your primary care doctor and specialists are in the plan.
  • What are your medications? Verify the plan's drug formulary to ensure it covers what you take.
  • How often do you use insurance? If you're healthy and rarely see a doctor, a low premium with higher copayments might work better for you.
  • Do you travel outside Florida? Consider a PPO or a plan with emergency coverage outside the state.
  • Do you have Medicaid? Dual Eligible Special Needs Plans (D-SNP) can give you almost total coverage.

Lopcha Tip

Don't focus only on the monthly premium. A plan with $0 premium but $50 specialist copays could cost much more than one with $30/month if you see specialists frequently. Analyze your actual use of medical services from the previous year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I have Medicare Advantage and Medicaid at the same time?

Yes. If you qualify for both, there are D-SNP (Dual Eligible Special Needs Plans) designed specifically for people with Medicare and Medicaid. These plans usually have $0 copayments for most services.

Can I go back to Original Medicare if I don't like my Advantage plan?

Yes, but you have specific windows to do so. The most important thing is that if you return to Original Medicare, it may be difficult to get a Medigap (Supplement) plan if you have pre-existing conditions, depending on the state.

Do Advantage plans change every year?

Yes. Insurers can change premiums, copayments, provider networks, and drug formularies every year. That's why it's important to review your plan every October before the AEP.
